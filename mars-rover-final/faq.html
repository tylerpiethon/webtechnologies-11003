Frequently Asked Questions

When did the rovers land on mars?

Perseverance landed on mars in 2021, February 18. Curiosity landed on mars in 2012, August 6th.

Are the rovers the same?

No. Although they are similar in design, Curiousity is for finding evidence of microbial life, while Perserverence collects samples as intact rock cores, each about the size of a piece of blackboard chalk, and stores them in metal tubes.

What did Curiosity discover?

Diverse organic molecules preserved in clay-rich rocks, some resembling DNA building blocks, suggesting that Mars once had the chemical ingredients for life.

What are the next steps for the rovers on mars?

Perseverance is currently collecting samples of Martian rock to study for the planet’s past habitability. It will also start preparing for future human missions. Curiosity has been working to decipher the ancient Martian climate and its transformation into the current desert landscape. And exploring regions on mars believed to have had underground water many years ago.
